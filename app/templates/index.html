WHO WILL REMEMBER

A Film about Armenia - 1915

WHO WILL REMEMBER "...the annihilation of the Armenians?" - A.D. 1939

Based on a screenplay written by Barbara Erysian - a second generation Armenian American.

Screenwriter Barbara Erysian grew up hearing the story from her Grandmother about surviving the Armenian crisis. Later on, in middle age, Barbara reflected on her Grandma Alice’s words, “Never forget.” Looking for a medium which could reach the most people in an entertaining way she chose to write this story as a film script. This will be Ms. Erysian’s first produced screenplay. She was born in Sacramento, California.

WE WILL REMEMBER

During the Armenian Genocide, a 16 year old girl taken to an orphanage converts to Islam until she can escape the Turkish soldiers trying to kill her and the children in her charge.

"This film is dedicated to my grandmother and her younger brother who escaped the Armenian Genocide of 1915 and to all the others who survived or perished. She kept him alive at an orphanage by gathering small wads of dough one breadcrumb at a time. They were rescued by a Christian man passing by who recognized the sign of the fish. They were reunited with their remaining family and immigrated to America." - Barbara Erysian
